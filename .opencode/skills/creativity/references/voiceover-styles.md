# Voiceover Styles Reference

5 core VO categories with delivery specifications.

## 1. Conversational Friendly

**Tone:** Warm, relatable, like chatting with a friend
**Delivery:** Natural pace, informal language, intimate feel
**Best For:** Lifestyle, community, how-to, approachable brands
**Example Brands:** Glossier, Airbnb, Casper

**Direction keywords:** warm, friendly, natural, relatable, intimate, casual, approachable, down-to-earth, genuine

**Pacing:** Moderate, natural pauses, breathing room
**Pitch:** Mid-range, varied naturally
**Energy:** Medium, engaged but not hyped

---

## 2. Authoritative Expert

**Tone:** Commanding, credible, professional, trusted
**Delivery:** Firm clarity, measured pace, confident assertions
**Best For:** Finance, legal, healthcare, B2B, professional services
**Example Brands:** McKinsey, Goldman Sachs, Mayo Clinic

**Direction keywords:** confident, credible, expert, authoritative, professional, knowledgeable, trustworthy, commanding, assured

**Pacing:** Measured, deliberate, unhurried
**Pitch:** Lower register, steady
**Energy:** Calm confidence, gravitas

---

## 3. Inspirational Motivational

**Tone:** Uplifting, aspirational, emotionally moving
**Delivery:** Dynamic energy, powerful cadence, belief-driven
**Best For:** Sports, wellness, self-help, non-profits, causes
**Example Brands:** Nike, Peloton, charity campaigns

**Direction keywords:** inspiring, powerful, uplifting, passionate, moving, aspirational, triumphant, emotional, motivating

**Pacing:** Building momentum, dynamic variation
**Pitch:** Rises through key moments
**Energy:** High at peaks, intimate at valleys

---

## 4. Formal Professional

**Tone:** Direct, professional, measured, serious
**Delivery:** Clear enunciation, structured, moderate pace
**Best For:** Corporate comms, compliance, training, institutional
**Example Brands:** IBM corporate, financial disclosures

**Direction keywords:** professional, formal, clear, direct, measured, proper, polished, corporate, institutional

**Pacing:** Even, predictable, structured
**Pitch:** Mid-range, minimal variation
**Energy:** Consistent, controlled, steady

---

## 5. Playful Humorous

**Tone:** Light, witty, amusing, energetic
**Delivery:** Quick pace, comic timing, personality-driven
**Best For:** Consumer goods, entertainment, youth brands
**Example Brands:** Old Spice, Skittles, Geico

**Direction keywords:** fun, witty, playful, energetic, cheeky, humorous, light, entertaining, quirky

**Pacing:** Quick, comedic beats, timing-dependent
**Pitch:** Varied for comedy effect
**Energy:** High, animated, expressive

---

## Voice Talent Considerations

**Gender:** Move beyond "announcer" default; match brand + audience
**Accent:** Diverse representation; match target market
**Age match:** Teens respond to youthful; adults to mature voices
**Consistency:** Same voice across campaigns increases recall
