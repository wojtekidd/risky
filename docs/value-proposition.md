## The Problem We Solve

Most organizations know business continuity matters. Few actually have it under control.

BCM software has existed for decades — and yet:

- Business Impact Analyses still take **weeks of chasing people** across email threads
- Plans live in **outdated Word documents** on someone's hard drive
- Executives get **no meaningful visibility** until an auditor asks for evidence
- IT teams are handed tools that demand they **rebuild their entire CMDB** before the software is useful
- When a real incident happens, the plan is either stale, missing, or nobody can find it

The tools built to solve this problem became part of the problem. Complex, expensive, and designed for a compliance checkbox — not for the people who have to use them every day.

**Risky is different.** We built business continuity software that works the way your organization actually works.

## How We Address the Biggest Pain Points

### "Our BIA process takes weeks and the data is stale before we finish."

**Risky's answer: Zero-Jargon Impact Wizard (F1) + Frictionless Delegation (F2)**

Traditional BIA requires business owners to understand RTO, MTPD, and MBCO before they can contribute a single data point. Most don't — so the BC Manager chases them for weeks.

Risky flips this. Business owners interact with plain-language sliders and visual impact tools that translate their answers into hard continuity metrics automatically. When technical depth is needed, magic links go directly to IT or HR — no accounts, no meetings, no delays. They open a link, enter what they know, and close the tab.

The result: a complete, accurate BIA in days, not weeks. And because inputs flow through a live system, the data stays fresh.

---

### "Our plans are scattered across SharePoint, email, and old documents nobody maintains."

**Risky's answer: Intelligent Asset Profiling (F4) + Dynamic Dependency Mapping (F3)**

Risky doesn't ask you to migrate a legacy CMDB or rebuild your asset inventory from scratch. It asks only for what business continuity actually needs.

Drag an asset into place. Type a hostname. Select the technology from a pre-built dictionary. Roles and ownership cascade down the architecture tree. The system builds a structured, version-controlled dependency model — automatically — without burdening your team with data entry work that doesn't serve a continuity purpose.

Everything lives in one place, maintained by the people closest to each layer, connected to the plans that depend on it.

---

### "Leadership has no idea what our risk exposure looks like until an auditor shows up."

**Risky's answer: Simple Management View (F5) — Asset Matrix, Concentration Heatmap, Deep Dives**

Risk reports that only BC Managers can read aren't risk reports — they're compliance artifacts.

Risky's management view translates your full architecture into a single interactive grid. Tiles darken as shared dependencies accumulate, turning hidden Single Points of Failure into something any executive can spot in thirty seconds. One click reveals exactly which business processes collapse if that asset fails.

No dashboards that require a two-hour orientation. No reports to request. Just a clear, always-current picture of operational exposure — built for the people who make decisions.

---

### "Our BC tool is so complex, only one person in the organization knows how to use it."

**Risky's design principle: Ruthlessly optimized for the job, not for feature count.**

Enterprise BCM platforms are built to handle every conceivable edge case across every regulated industry. That ambition produces tools that require months of implementation, dedicated administrators, and external consultants to set up.

Risky is designed for the outcome, not the specification sheet. Every screen, every workflow, every data field exists because it directly serves business continuity — nothing else. New users are productive in hours. Organizations without a dedicated BC team can still run a credible program.

---

### "We can't explain our risk scores to auditors."

**Risky's answer: Transparent risk logic, built for accountability.**

AI-powered risk scoring is only useful if you can stand behind the output. Black-box models that can't be explained create as many audit problems as they solve.

Risky exposes the logic behind every risk assessment — what data fed the score, what assumptions it rests on, and where human judgement was applied. When a regulator or auditor asks why a process was classified as critical, your BC Manager has a clear, documented answer. Not a vendor-supplied algorithm no one can open.

## Value by Buyer Persona

### For the BCM Program Owner
*"Stop managing continuity in spreadsheets."*

Risky centralizes your BIA, dependency maps, plans, and audit trail in one system. Magic-link delegation means you stop being the bottleneck for every data collection cycle. Your plans stay current because the people closest to the data maintain their own layer — without needing an account.

### For the Chief Risk Officer
*"See your real exposure — not a report someone prepared last quarter."*

The management view gives you a live, enterprise-wide picture of operational dependencies and concentration risk. Risk scores are explainable. Regulatory coverage is built in. When the board asks about resilience posture, you have an answer backed by current data, not a dated document.

### For the CIO / IT Leader
*"Your team maps what they know. The platform handles the rest."*

Risky meets your IT team where they are — no CMDB rebuild required. Dependency mapping supports both top-down (application → infrastructure) and bottom-up (hardware → business process) approaches. Ownership cascades intelligently. The platform captures only the continuity-relevant data your team needs to maintain, not a full inventory of everything you've ever deployed.

### For the Compliance / GRC Officer
*"Audit-ready from day one."*

Every plan update is versioned. Every BIA cycle is documented. Every risk score is traceable to its inputs. When your auditor arrives, you open Risky — not a file server.

### For the CFO
*"One unplanned outage costs more than a year of Risky."*

The question isn't whether BC software is worth the investment. It's whether the tool you choose actually gets adopted and maintained. Risky's low implementation overhead and accessible UX mean the program runs continuously — not just at audit time. That's what delivers the ROI.
